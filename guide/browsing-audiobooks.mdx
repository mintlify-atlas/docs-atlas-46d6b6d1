Aradia provides multiple ways to discover audiobooks from LibriVox's extensive collection of public domain content. Whether you're looking for a specific title or browsing by mood, you'll find what you need.

## Browse by Genre

The home screen features a grid of popular genres to help you start exploring:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access the genre grid">
    Scroll down on the home screen to the "Browse Genres" section. You'll see genre chips including Adventure, Biography, Children, Comedy, Fantasy, Horror, Humor, Love, Mystery, Philosophy, Poetry, Religion, Romance, Sci-Fi, and War.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select a genre">
    Tap any genre chip to view all audiobooks in that category. The app will display a scrollable list of audiobooks filtered by your selected genre.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Browse the results">
    Scroll through the audiobooks, which display cover art, title, author, and download statistics. Tap any audiobook to view its details.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You can also tap on subject tags within an audiobook's detail page to explore related content.
</Tip>

## Search for Audiobooks

Use the search feature to find specific titles, authors, or subjects:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open search">
    Tap the search icon in the app's bottom navigation bar to access the search screen.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose your search filter">
    Select one of three filter options:
    - **Title**: Search by book name (e.g., "Pride and Prejudice")
    - **Author**: Search by creator name (e.g., "Jane Austen")
    - **Subjects**: Search by topic or genre (e.g., "Romance, Fiction")
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter your search query">
    Type your search term in the search bar. For multiple terms, separate them with commas (e.g., "Mystery, Detective").
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Submit your search">
    Tap the search button or press enter. Results will appear as a scrollable list showing matching audiobooks.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  When searching with multiple comma-separated terms, the app will find audiobooks matching ANY of your terms (OR logic).
</Note>

## Curated Collections

The home screen displays several curated sections to help you discover content:

<Accordion title="Popular All Time">
  Browse the most downloaded audiobooks from LibriVox's entire catalog. This section showcases timeless classics that listeners love.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Trending This Week">
  Discover what other listeners are currently enjoying. This section updates regularly to show recently popular titles.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Recommended for You">
  Based on your selected genre preferences (chosen during onboarding or updated in settings), the app displays personalized audiobook recommendations.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Recently Played">
  Quickly resume audiobooks you've started listening to. This section appears when you have playback history and shows your most recent listens.
</Accordion>

## Filter by Language

Aradia supports over 20 languages from LibriVox's collection:

1. Go to **Settings** from the home screen
2. Tap **Visible languages**
3. Select the languages you want to see in your audiobook listings
4. Tap **Save**

Available languages include English, Deutsch (German), Español (Spanish), Français (French), Nederlands (Dutch), Português (Portuguese), Italiano (Italian), Русский (Russian), 日本語 (Japanese), 中文 (Chinese), and many more.

<Tip>
  If no languages are selected, all audiobooks will be shown regardless of language.
</Tip>

## View Audiobook Details

When you find an audiobook that interests you:

1. **Tap the audiobook** in any list or grid
2. View comprehensive details including:
   - Cover artwork
   - Author and narrator information
   - Download statistics and ratings
   - Full description
   - Individual chapter/file listings with durations
   - Subject tags for related content
3. **Take action** using the controls on the detail page:
   - Add to favorites (heart icon)
   - Download for offline listening
   - Start playback immediately

<Note>
  Each audiobook shows its origin (LibriVox, YouTube, or local import) and displays relevant metadata like ratings and download counts for LibriVox content.
</Note>
